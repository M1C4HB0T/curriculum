Asking Effective Programming Questions

Learn how to ask well-formed questions in coding communities to get the best help possible.

Introduction

Throughout your programming journey, you will undoubtedly find yourself seeking assistance from online coding communities, such as our own, or engaging in discussions with colleagues. While the act of asking questions may appear straightforward, it is crucial in a programming environment to pose well-formed questions that provide sufficient context. This approach will maximize the value you derive from requesting help. Essentially, the goal is to “help others help you.” This lesson is designed to equip you with the skills necessary to effectively ask questions in programming communities, such as those on Discord, Stack Overflow, or within your workplace.

Tips for Obtaining the Best Assistance

Always Include Your Code and Relevant Context

When requesting help, it is essential to share your code, error messages, terminal output, server logs, or any other pertinent details. By providing as much context as possible and identifying the specific problem area—such as a particular function or line of code—you increase the likelihood of receiving helpful assistance.

Failing to provide relevant information can significantly hinder the troubleshooting process. Without clear context, the person helping you may need to engage in back-and-forth exchanges to clarify the issue, which could lead to frustration on both sides.

Focus on the Problem, Not the Solution

Many learners tend to ask how to directly solve a specific task or assignment. However, it is important to recognize that the purpose of such exercises is to help you develop problem-solving skills, including how to approach a task.

Rather than asking for a solution, it is more effective to ask about specific challenges you are encountering. For example:

"I’m trying to return a string that displays the winner in Rock Paper Scissors, but I’m encountering a syntax error on line 12. Can you help me resolve this? Here’s my code."

Don’t Take Requests for More Context Personally

Remember that individuals who volunteer in coding communities are there to assist you! A question that seems clear and “obvious” to you may not appear that way to someone with more experience. An expert often has a broader understanding of potential issues and edge cases, and may request additional context to ensure they guide you in the right direction.

If you are asked to provide further details, trust that there is likely a valid reason. Experienced volunteers are dedicated to helping, and they will ask for more information when necessary.
